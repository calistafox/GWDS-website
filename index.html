Coding Worksheets

The coding worksheets begun at the very basics, first taking us through creating a pure HTML website, learning about adding content and seeing how the different elements react before we add our owns styling. We also set up GitHub and looked into the workflow of storing your code online. In the following weeks we used CSS to style the webpage, adding colours, styling elements, defining fonts, CSS selectors and used flexbox to organise content. The final project developed my skills in flexbox, first using it to make the navigation appear horizontally, with some aligned to the left and some to the right margin. Also using it to centre content and align it next to each other. In next weeks worksheet we styled it further, making a button link to an external html document, animating buttons and imported google and adobe fonts. Lastly, I completed the optional carousel, where I styled 5 images be in a horizonal row using flex, which can be navigated through like a slideshow using a scroll bar. After completing these worksheets, I now have a good understanding of how a website is built and look forward to applying these skills for my final website.

Dilatational

Dilatational typographic systems curve text and elements dynamically around a central point. The circular composition invites the eye to spiral inward, drawing in the viewer. I drew inspiration from Rinko Kawauchi’s M/E collection, particularly the image featuring two children where the sun reflects in a circle through the camera lens. This felt powerful as it invites the viewer to pause and connect, creating emotion. It inspired me to apply a similar technique in my design, allowing the circles of text to pull the reader in. I chose Clarendon as the font because it is easily read, ensuring the text remains easily comprehensible despite the potentially distorting typographic system.

Radial

Radial designs use a central focal point, radiating all content out from that centre. Researching this typographic system reminded me of recent flower sketches I had done. I developed these sketches further and used the pen tool in InDesign to create shapes for my design. I wanted to incorporate nature into one of my designs as this is a strong theme through the exhibition, and felt the words radiating from a flower further pushed the feeling created from a radial composition. The clean style of the Komet font complements the harsh vector art used in the design.

Random

Random typographic systems arrange elements without a defined pattern, aim, or purpose, creating a playful experience while viewers search for connections. I particularly enjoy designs where text flowed in multiple directions, where the eye keeps getting more from the design the longer it looks.

Grid

Grid systems organise text and graphics into columns and rows, putting readability first. I begun my design with a 3 x 4 grid to structure my content, allowing the white space on the space to guide the viewers eyes down the page. Futura PT’s clean, modern aesthetic aligned well with the ensure, continuing to priorities readability.

Axial

Axial designs organize content along an imaginary axis, which can be straight or curved. Content can be symmetrical or asymmetrical, and multiple axes can guide the viewer’s eye to create dynamic compositions. Inspired by an image from Rinko Kawauchi’s Illuminance collection, where sunlight breaks through trees, I explored the natural juxtaposition of soft organic shapes and harsh axis lines. After some sketching, I decided on a dandelion as the centrepiece, which encapsulates the themes of the exhibition while directing the reader’s eye down the page. I used Adobe Garamond Pro for its bold yet clear composition, contrasting with the delicate flower vector.

Modular

Modular layouts use repeating elements, such as shapes, to structure content. My design uses eight squares to contain and separate the text, taking the viewers eye down the right side of the design. I used Clarendon font to in keep with the clean feel.

Transitional

Transitional compositions aim to create hold natural & dynamic movement within their compositions, often mirroring the layering of sediment and stone. This was the most challenging composition for me as I struggled to create a sense of things flowing in my design. I used the type on a path tool to give my text movement.

Bilateral

Bilateral systems align content along a single axis, often creating symmetrical designs. For my composition, I used a diagonal axis, aligning all text except the title to the right side. This creates visual interest while maintaining balance and readability.
